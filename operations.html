Overview

The operational backbone of Cacao Cellars: the software stack that handles sales, inventory, and books, and the licensing pathway that lets us serve alcohol and play music legally. Both are essential and both have a fixed cost — understanding what each costs to set up and what each costs to run is part of getting opening day right.

The software stack is Toast + Shopify + QuickBooks, connected by a lightweight middleware app (Shogo or similar). Each tool does what it's best at — Toast for cafe operations and tipped wages, Shopify for retail and online sales, QuickBooks for clean books and tax compliance. Total cost ~$7,500 upfront and $5,000/year ongoing.

Licensing breaks into two tracks: NC ABC permits (run through a consultant like Vickie West, ~$1,200 fee, 4–6 weeks after lease signing) and performance-rights music licensing (ASCAP and BMI required even for original music or just playing the radio aloud). Total ~$4,000 to get licensed at opening, ~$2,500 annually thereafter.

Sequencing

Both tracks start at lease signing and run in parallel. Software contracts can be signed earlier (Toast onboarding takes 4–6 weeks itself) and Shopify can be set up at any time. Liquor licensing is the harder constraint — we cannot accept alcohol delivery without it, and the timeline does not compress no matter how prepared we are.

  1. Week 0 — Lease signing. Both clocks start.
  2. Weeks 1–2 — Software contracts. Toast hardware order, Shopify store setup, QuickBooks Online subscription, middleware connector configuration.
  3. Weeks 1–6 — ABC licensing. Consultant engaged, application submitted, Durham PD local review.
  4. Pre-opening — Music licensing signed. ASCAP and BMI annual fees set up before any music plays.
  5. Opening day — All license certificates posted as required by NC code.
