A 1,683 sq ft wine and chocolate bar built around Tier 1 chocolate production, a curated wine program, and Sunday brunch. Click any room in the layout below to explore its design.

The Concept

A Wine & Chocolate Bar in Durham

Salvaged brick, hand-carved Balinese wood, a glass-walled chocolate atelier, and a wine program built around the pairing flight. The atmosphere we’re building toward, room by room.

The Front of House

The Wine Bar

Exposed brick, an 18-foot bar with a backlit bottle wall, communal teak tables, and rattan stools. Eight bar seats facing the chocolate atelier through a viewing window.

Tier 1 Production

The Chocolate Atelier

A glass-walled prep room visible from the dining floor. Tier 1 production — melting, tempering, molding, and packaging — happening in plain view of every guest.

The Signature

Pairings & Tasting Flights

Three or four wines poured beside an equal number of bonbons, each pairing chosen to bring out what neither does alone. The signature experience of the room.

Outdoor Seating

The Patio

Salvaged-brick walls, low ambient lighting, café tables and lush plantings, with the carved Balinese doorway as the threshold between street and room.

Running the Business

The systems and licenses behind the room — what makes opening day possible and the months after sustainable.

POS · e-commerce · accounting

Software Stack

Three best-in-class systems connected by middleware: Toast for cafe operations and tipped wages, Shopify for retail and online sales, QuickBooks Online for books and tax. Toast wins the Lightspeed comparison because Shopify already handles the retail strength Lightspeed would otherwise provide — what's left is the operational backbone, where Toast's restaurant DNA matters most. Budget $7,500 upfront, $5,000/year ongoing.

Liquor & Music Licenses

NC ABC permitting through Vickie West (NC ABC Consulting Services) at $1,200; 4–6 weeks after lease signing. ASCAP and BMI music licensing required even if we only play original music or the radio. Three consultants under review, Durham Police Department local liaison identified. Total ~$4,000 to get licensed, ~$2,500 annually thereafter.
