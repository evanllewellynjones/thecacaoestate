Chocolate Production Lead

Part-time · 25–30 hours/week · Tier 1 production · Founding hire

Back to Employees

About Cacao Cellars

Cacao Cellars is a 1,450 sq ft wine and chocolate bar opening in downtown Durham. We pair house-made chocolate with a thoughtfully curated wine, fortified, and spirits program in a warm, Bali-inspired space. Our menu is chocolate-first: every house-made chocolate is the hero of the experience, and the beverage program is built around it.

This is a founding-team role. We are hiring a chocolatier who wants creative input and a sustainable schedule — not the 60-hour grind of a hotel pastry kitchen.

The Role

The Chocolate Production Lead owns daily chocolate production for the bar — tempering, molding, finishing, and quality control across our six-piece signature lineup, plus seasonal and limited-edition pieces. We run a Tier 1 operation: pre-made Callebaut and Valrhona callets, tempered and molded into our custom CC-monogrammed pieces.

Estimated production volume is 100–150 finished pieces per night across 4–6 active varieties — comfortably within a 25–30 hour weekly schedule with structured prep days. You will work in a dedicated chocolate prep room (8′ × 10′, climate-controlled to 65–70°F at 40–50% relative humidity) with reach-in refrigeration, a marble work surface, and a tempering setup specified to your preferred workflow.

Specifically, you will:

  • Run daily tempering and molding production using Callebaut callets and Valrhona for premium pieces.
  • Maintain quality control on temper, gloss, snap, and shelf life — every piece that leaves the prep room represents the brand.
  • Manage chocolate inventory, callet ordering, and waste tracking against COGS targets.
  • Partner on recipe development for seasonal varieties and limited-edition pieces — your creative input is welcomed within our defined chocolate-first menu.
  • Maintain ServSafe-compliant prep practices, equipment, and the production log.
  • Optionally lead chocolate-making classes during off-hours (Monday/Tuesday daytime), with revenue share if you want to take that on.

Compensation

Compensation is set based on experience. The role is hourly, part-time, 25–30 hours per week. Daytime production schedule with flexibility — Tuesday through Saturday mornings/afternoons are typical.

Who We Are Looking For

Required

  • Hands-on tempering experience — you can temper Callebaut from cold callets without hesitation, using seeding, tabling, or a tempering machine, whichever you prefer.
  • Polycarbonate molding workflow — coating, capping, and releasing molded chocolates without bloom, air pockets, or gloss issues.
  • ServSafe Food Handler or Manager certification, or willing to obtain pre-opening (we will reimburse).
  • Comfort working independently for stretches — most production shifts will be solo with the owner or GM available.
  • Reliability for a fixed weekly production rhythm — chocolate is a working calendar, not a creative whim.

Strongly preferred

  • ACF Certified Pastry Cook or equivalent credential.
  • Experience with colored cocoa butter, transfer sheets, and hand-finishing techniques.
  • Front-of-house comfort and pairing curiosity — occasional guest interaction during retail pickup or class hosting.
  • Callebaut Academy, École Chocolat, or comparable formal chocolate training.
  • Familiarity with shelf-life management, water activity, and packaging for retail.

Not a fit if

  • Your background is cake decoration without explicit tempering and molding experience — different muscle, different craft.
  • You expect full creative authorship of the chocolate menu — Cacao Cellars has a defined six-piece lineup at opening, with collaborative recipe development from there.
  • You need executive pastry chef hours and salary ($65K+) — this role is intentionally scoped to part-time hourly with a clear growth path.

Schedule

Production hours are typically Tuesday through Saturday between 9 AM and 4 PM, with the specific weekly pattern set with you based on production needs and your availability. Mondays are typically used for deep-clean, ordering, and recipe development. Evenings and Sunday brunch service are not your shift — front of house handles the bar.

Prep Room Conditions 65–70°F at 40–50% relative humidity, dedicated mechanical, separate from the wash area. We are building this room properly because we know you cannot temper chocolate in a humid kitchen.

How to Apply

Send the following to [your email] with the subject line “Chocolate Production Lead — [Your Name]”:

  • A resume or CV with chocolate-specific experience highlighted.
  • 3–5 photos of your work — molded pieces, finished bonbons, or production setups. Phone photos are fine; we care about temper, gloss, and consistency, not photography.
  • A short note (one paragraph is enough) on which chocolate you are most proud of having made and why.
  • Two professional references.

Process: Initial conversation by phone or video, then a paid 90-minute working interview in our prep space — temper Callebaut, mold one variety, finish it. Pay $100 for the working session. Reference calls and offer follow.

We aim to hire 6–8 weeks before opening so you have time to develop the production playbook, dial in your tempering setup in our space, and pre-produce opening inventory.

A note on this role Chocolatiers with the skill set we want are usually working in pastry kitchens that demand more hours than the work deserves. We are scoping this deliberately at part-time with a real wage, real benefits, real creative input, and a path to grow. If you have been looking for a chocolate role that respects the craft and your time, we would like to talk to you.
